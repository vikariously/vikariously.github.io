Makes 4 servings
 
## Ingredients

### For the Cauliflower

- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 2 teaspoons garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon pepper
- 1/2 cup LOW FAT milk or milk alternative
- 1 head cauliflower
- ⅓ cup Panko or breadcrumbs

### For the glaze

- ¼ cup of your favorite buffalo sauce (we use a Frank’s Tiddles Remix -- see recipe below)
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil of your choice (we usually use olive)
- 1 tablespoon honey (can double if you’re worried about heat)

## Preparation

1. Preheat the oven to 450˚F (230˚C). Oil/spray a cookie sheet HEAVILY (these are very likely going to get stuck on the pan, but its okay. PAM is my recommendation, second is parchment paper.)
2. In a large bowl, add the flour, paprika, garlic powder, salt, pepper, and milk, and stir until well-combined.
3. Break the head of cauliflower into florets, about 1½-inches wide. Add the cauliflower florets to the batter, making sure each piece is evenly coated. This is when you add the panko/bread crumbs SLOWLY (1-2 T at a time) and make sure the batter is not wet, drippy, or clumpy before you put it on the baking sheet. 
4. Arrange the coated cauliflower on the baking sheet. Bake for 20 minutes, flipping halfway after ten minutes.
5. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine the buffalo sauce, oil, and honey and stir until evenly combined. Brush the buffalo sauce mixture on the cauliflower and bake for another 20 minutes.
6. I like to add a little more of the buffalo/hot sauce mixture on top with some green onions

## Frank’s Tiddles Remix Buffalo Sauce
- 1 part Frank’s Red Hot Original
- 1 part Frank’s Buffalo Sauce (we prefer “Standard” over “Extra Hot”)

Combine and store in a squeeze bot
